# 40s and Blunts Weekly Rolling Coverage
### Week 14 · The one where Bip Roberts stopped pretending to be modest about it

---

**MATCHUP OF THE WEEK**

Yahoo designated Bip Roberts def. The Chicago Way, 9–1, as the matchup of the week, which is either an editorial choice or an automated system that also has feelings about this. The Chicago Way came in as the second-ranked team in the league, held Bip to exactly one category — stolen bases — and lost nine of the other ten. Chris Sale moved $10M in the right direction. It did not matter. The Chicago Way has spent weeks being quietly excellent, and this was the week the quiet stopped working.

**LAST WEEK'S RESULTS**

**Chief Noc-A-Homa def. MayTheSchwarBeWithU, 8–2.** The commissioner swept every offensive category and every pitching category and left MayTheSchwarBeWithU the two that remain when everything else is gone: stolen bases and saves plus holds. There is a version of this matchup where it's competitive. This was not it.

**Bad MoFo's def. Old Fashioned, 7–3.** Bad MoFo's wins a category-league matchup 7–3, which is the kind of sentence you write down and read back a few times. It happened. Old Fashioned took ERA, WHIP, and quality starts, suggesting a functional pitching staff is indeed deployed somewhere in that roster, and no one is home to watch it work.

**Bell Biv Devo def. Stone Jack Ballers, 6–4.** Stone Jack took home runs, RBI, strikeouts, and saves plus holds — the categories associated with effort and aggression — and lost the week anyway, ceding runs, stolen bases, OPS, and the entire pitching ratio column to a team that entered this matchup below the playoff line. Stone Jack will have some thoughts about this. He probably already does.

**Bip Roberts def. The Chicago Way, 9–1.** Covered above. We have reviewed this result and can confirm it is accurate.

**Grand Theft Votto def. Thurgood Jenkins, 5-5.** A tie, rendered as a win for Grand Theft Votto on the strength of taking five categories to Thurgood's five. Thurgood won home runs, RBI, OPS, strikeouts, and saves plus holds. Grand Theft Votto won runs, steals, ERA, WHIP, and quality starts. Both teams walked away having won exactly half of everything, and Grand Theft Votto gets credit in the standings because the system needs a winner. The system, like all systems, made a choice and committed to it.

**L-A-Loosh def. Section 34, 4-4-2.** Two categories tied, four categories apiece, and what emerges is a 4–4–2 line that officially counts as a win for L-A-Loosh. Section 34 took the power and the ratios; L-A-Loosh took the speed and the strikeouts and the saves. It is July in three days. L-A-Loosh is 10 games under .500. Sixth in June feels like ranking a slow-building thunderstorm "currently not raining."

**QUESTIONABLE ACTIVITY**

The week began with Bad MoFo's dropping Edward Cabrera on Saturday morning, and by Sunday afternoon they had added Rico Garcia and Fernando Cruz in consecutive minutes, suggesting a philosophy of volume over discernment. Rico Garcia then lost $10M in value by end of week. The receipt was left in the bag.

Thurgood Jenkins added and dropped Stephen Kolek within 14 hours, which is a transaction that exists in the record but produced no apparent effect on the material universe. He then added Andre Pallante, then Kevin Kelly, then Mickey Moniak, then Eduard Bazardo across the week — a different kind of transacting than Stone Jack's, more reactive, less strategic, though the distinction may be academic when you're five and a half games out.

Section 34 dropped Michael King and added Eric Lauer, which was either a rotation decision or a mistake, depending on how Eric Lauer's next several starts go.

Stone Jack Ballers added TJ Rumfield, dropped TJ Rumfield 70 minutes later, added Gage Jump, and then — beginning at midnight Sunday and running through 3:24 AM — added Jake McCarthy, dropped Jake McCarthy, added Shane Bieber, and added Emerson Hancock, who had just been released by Chief Noc-A-Homa six days prior. Stone Jack lost this week 6–4. The midnight transacting is consistent with someone who is working on it.

Bip Roberts added Carson Benge on Monday, dropped Carson Benge three days later, and on Sunday added Theo Gillen, George Lombard Jr., Jackson Jobe, and Nick Pivetta in a 22-minute window. The team won 9–1. The moves came after the win. Bip Roberts is tidying up.

The Chicago Way was quiet most of the week and then dropped three players in 22 minutes on Sunday, adding Hunter Gaddis and Luis García Jr. A late-week correction from the team that got corrected 9–1 on the scoreboard. Things happen in order.

Old Fashioned made no moves.

**The Salary-Cap Sucker — Stone Jack Ballers: 14.5 wins per $100M ($497M payroll, 72 wins).** Nearly half a billion dollars, a 6–4 loss to Bell Biv Devo, and a 3 AM add of Emerson Hancock. The commitment is not in question.

**The $40M Ghost — Mike Trout (Grand Theft Votto): down $10M this week.** The $40M contract is now a $30M contract, which is still a lot of money for someone who helped lose the salary-efficiency award by a significant margin. Grand Theft Votto has $76M on the IL and their most expensive active player just got cheaper. The week had a theme.
