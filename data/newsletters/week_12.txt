MATCHUP OF THE WEEK

Stone Jack Ballers def. The Chicago Way, 6–4. Yahoo designated this the Matchup of the Week, which the commissioner's brother will not let the commissioner forget. Stone Jack took the pitching side clean — ERA, WHIP, QS, and SV+H — then grabbed HR and RBI to cover the offense. Chicago Way answered with R, SB, OPS, and K, which on any other week feels like a fine haul. This week it was four. Stone Jack continues to play with an urgency that is either inspiring or exhausting depending on your roster position. The siblings are currently separated by eight games in the standings. Someone should probably mention that at dinner.

Grand Theft Votto def. MayTheSchwarBeWithU, 5–5. A tie in points, separated by tiebreaker. Grand Theft Votto took the pitching side; MayTheSchwarBeWithU took the offense and nothing else. This is the most 5–5 a 5–5 has ever felt.

Stone Jack Ballers def. The Chicago Way, 6–4. *(See Matchup of the Week, above.)*

Thurgood Jenkins def. L-A-Loosh, 8–2. Thurgood took eight categories. L-A-Loosh won SB and K, which at least suggests the roster is still breathing. It is June, L-A-Loosh is hovering near last place, and the league will continue to pretend this is fine until September when it suddenly isn't.

QUESTIONABLE ACTIVITY

Bell Biv Devo had a week. At 4:16 AM on Tuesday, they added Gabriel Moreno and dropped Clayton Beeter — reasonable enough. By 7:58 AM the same morning they had also dropped J.T. Realmuto, presumably in their sleep. Then on Thursday they added Willy Adames, held him for three days, dropped him Sunday morning, and replaced him with Taylor Ward in the span of a single minute. The same Sunday, they added Shota Imanaga and dropped him 60 seconds later, before landing on Jeffrey Springs. Bell Biv Devo won their matchup this week. We cannot explain the causal relationship.

MayTheSchwarBeWithU added Noah Cameron on Saturday morning and dropped him Sunday morning, adding Michael McGreevy in his place. Also added Luis Lara on Saturday, dropped her Sunday in favor of Blaze Alexander. Two full roster refreshes in 24 hours after losing a tiebreaker. The stages of grief, rendered as waiver claims.

Bad MoFo's added Ranger Suárez, Brandon Marsh, and Luke Raley on Friday afternoon — three moves in two minutes, dropping Santana, Swanson, and Steer to make room. Points for urgency. The timing, one day before the week ended, is a note about optimism.

Bip Roberts dropped Jose Altuve. No replacement was logged in the data. Just dropped him. Moving on.

The Chicago Way added Colton Cowser and then, three minutes later, added Ezequiel Duran while dropping Bryson Stott. Efficient. Clean. Suspiciously calm about the whole thing, per usual.

L-A-Loosh dropped Garrett Whitlock and added Cole Carrigg. A reliever out, an outfielder in, the week after losing 8–2. The direction of travel is noted without comment.

Section 34 and Grand Theft Votto each made one quiet, sensible move. Nothing to see here, which in this company is its own kind of accomplishment.
