# 40s and Blunts Weekly Rolling Coverage
### Week 12 · The one where Bell Biv Devo discovered and then un-discovered several players

---

**MATCHUP OF THE WEEK**

Yahoo designated Stone Jack Ballers over The Chicago Way, 6–4, as the matchup of the week, and there is a reasonable case for it. The Chicago Way is second in the overall standings and has the quiet, load-bearing competence of a team that rarely loses close games. Stone Jack Ballers beat them anyway, sweeping the pitching side entirely — ERA, WHIP, QS, and SV+H — while adding HR and RBI on the hitting side. The Chicago Way took runs, steals, OPS, and strikeouts, which is a fine haul that happened to be one fewer category than they needed. The loss does nothing to The Chicago Way's playoff standing and probably very little to their confidence, but it is the kind of result that lingers in the back of a scoreboard.

---

**LAST WEEK'S RESULTS**

**Chief Noc-A-Homa def. Section 34, 7–3.** The commissioner swept seven categories, including every pitching line that mattered. Section 34 fought back with RBI, OPS, and ERA — the exact three categories a team wins when the other team has already won the matchup. A fine effort in service of nothing in particular.

**Bell Biv Devo def. Bad MoFo's, 6–4.** Bad MoFo's took ERA and WHIP, which is the kind of week where your pitching carries you and your pitching still isn't enough. Bell Biv Devo won HR, RBI, OPS, K, QS, and SV+H. We will note, without further comment, that Bell Biv Devo is currently ninth in the standings.

**Bip Roberts def. Old Fashioned, 8–1–1.** Old Fashioned won QS. Bip Roberts won everything else. There are only ten categories in a matchup, and Bip took eight of them. The tie in saves is the one piece of evidence that Old Fashioned's roster is still receiving baseball signals.

**Grand Theft Votto def. MayTheSchwarBeWithU, 5–5.** A tie counts as a win when Grand Theft Votto holds the tiebreaker, which appears to be how this went. Grand Theft Votto handled the pitching side cleanly; MayTheSchwarBeWithU countered with the power and saves categories and, ultimately, half the points. A 5–5 final. There is nothing wrong with a 5–5 matchup. It resolves neatly, like a form filed correctly.

**Stone Jack Ballers def. The Chicago Way, 6–4.** Covered above, at Yahoo's specific request.

**Thurgood Jenkins def. L-A-Loosh, 8–2.** Thurgood Jenkins won eight categories, including every hitting category except stolen bases and every pitching category without exception. L-A-Loosh won steals and strikeouts and called it a week. It is June, which is still technically early, and L-A-Loosh has nine championship banners and zero apparent urgency about any of this. The league is keeping one eye open.

**PLAYOFF PICTURE**

Six teams in, six teams out, and the top six are reasonably dug in. Chief Noc-A-Homa leads the overall standings by a margin that can only be described as deliberate. The Chicago Way, Stone Jack Ballers, Bad MoFo's, Bip Roberts, and Section 34 round out the field, with the last of them sitting four games clear of the cut line. That is a meaningful cushion — not comfortable, but comfortable enough that Section 34 can afford a bad week without checking their pulse.

Below the line, Thurgood Jenkins is the closest thing to a legitimate threat, sitting four back with ten weeks remaining. Grand Theft Votto is six back; the rest are eight-and-a-half or worse. Ten weeks is roughly one hundred category decisions, so none of this is formally over, but L-A-Loosh at eight-and-a-half back in June is a reminder that the playoffs are not yet an entitlement this year, dynasty or not.

Old Fashioned is fifteen back. No further analysis will be offered on that.

**QUESTIONABLE ACTIVITY**

The week's most notable transactor was **Bell Biv Devo**, who appears to have used the waiver wire the way most people use a dressing room — trying things on, reconsidering, and leaving the same way they came in. The sequence: added Gabriel Moreno on Tuesday morning, then dropped J.T. Realmuto four hours later, then added Willy Adames, then three days later dropped Adames, added Taylor Ward, added Shota Imanaga, dropped Shota Imanaga ninety seconds later, and added Jeffrey Springs. Imanaga was rostered for one minute and forty-three seconds by our count. This is not a criticism. It is a transcript.

**MayTheSchwarBeWithU** made five moves between Friday morning and Saturday, including adding Noah Cameron and then dropping Noah Cameron the following day in favor of Michael McGreevy. Cameron had approximately eighteen hours on the roster. This is the kind of thing that happens when you are trying to solve an ERA problem by Saturday afternoon and the available options are what they are.

**Bad MoFo's** made three adds on Friday — Ranger Suarez, Brandon Marsh, and Luke Raley — dropping Dansby Swanson, Spencer Steer, and Dennis Santana in the process. A full restructuring at the deadline of a week they were already losing. The moves may or may not help going forward. The scoreboard from this week has already been filed.

**Bip Roberts** dropped Jose Altuve. No replacement was logged in the data provided. The league noticed.

**The Chicago Way** added Colton Cowser and Ezequiel Duran, dropped Bryson Stott, and made it all look like something they had planned in advance. That tracks.
