# 40s and Blunts Weekly Rolling Coverage
### Week 12 · The one where Bell Biv Devo had opinions about their own roster

---

**MATCHUP OF THE WEEK**

Yahoo's algorithm, which has seen things, designated **Stone Jack Ballers def. The Chicago Way, 6–4** as the week's marquee event. Fitting. Stone Jack took HR, RBI, ERA, WHIP, QS, and SV+H — basically the whole pitching side of the ballot plus enough offense to make it uncomfortable. The Chicago Way answered with R, SB, OPS, and K, which is a fine haul that nevertheless lost the week. The bigger story may be in the standings: Stone Jack is now third, and The Chicago Way, who spent most of this season looking unbeatable from a distance, has lost a step. The gap between first and third is now eight games in the aggregate. That's not nothing.

**LAST WEEK'S RESULTS**

**Chief Noc-A-Homa def. Section 34, 7–3.** The commissioner swept seven categories including all three pitching ratio columns, added steals and runs for good measure, and generally behaved like a team that has no intention of losing first place quietly. Section 34 salvaged RBI, OPS, and ERA, which is a reasonable consolation prize and also three categories that have no impact on the final score. Chief Noc-A-Homa remains at the top of the league with the composure of a man who has never once checked his roster in a panic.

**Bell Biv Devo def. Bad MoFo's, 6–4.** Somewhat against the odds. Bell Biv Devo took HR, RBI, OPS, K, QS, and SV+H; Bad MoFo's countered with R, SB, ERA, and WHIP, which is its own kind of small miracle. That Bad MoFo's won the pitching ratios while also sitting fourth in the overall standings is a sentence the league will need to sit with for a moment.

**Bip Roberts def. Old Fashioned, 8–1–1.** Bip swept eight categories, tied saves, and lost only QS — which Old Fashioned won, presumably without knowing about it. Eight-to-one is a number usually associated with parking ticket violations, not fantasy baseball. Old Fashioned currently leads the league in Quality Starts won against Bip Roberts, which is the most Old Fashioned statistic imaginable.

**Grand Theft Votto def. MayTheSchwarBeWithU, 5–5.** A tie in the final count, resolved in Grand Theft Votto's favor on tiebreakers. Grand Theft Votto took steals, strikeouts, and swept the pitching ratios including QS. MayTheSchwarBeWithU took R, HR, RBI, OPS, and SV+H — five real categories — and still didn't win the week. There is a lesson in there about ERA management that MayTheSchwarBeWithU's subsequent transaction history suggests went unlearned.

**Stone Jack Ballers def. The Chicago Way, 6–4.** See above. The Matchup of the Week section already handled this one. Moving on.

**Thurgood Jenkins def. L-A-Loosh, 8–2.** L-A-Loosh took stolen bases and strikeouts. Thurgood Jenkins took everything else, including ERA and WHIP, which historically have belonged to L-A-Loosh when it felt like showing up. The nine championship banners were unavailable for comment. It is June. The sleeping bear theory remains intact, technically, but it took a shot this week.

**QUESTIONABLE ACTIVITY**

**Bell Biv Devo** had the week's most ambitious roster management, in the sense that it generated the most paperwork. Dropped Shota Imanaga, added Shota Imanaga. Dropped Willy Adames, added Willy Adames. In between: picked up Jeffrey Springs, Taylor Ward, and Gabriel Moreno, and parted with J.T. Realmuto and Clayton Beeter. This is either a sophisticated churn strategy or what happens when someone stress-refreshes the waiver wire at 11 p.m. on a Tuesday. Bell Biv Devo won this week, so we are legally required to drop the subject.

**MayTheSchwarBeWithU** made six transactions, including adding Luis Lara, dropping Luis Lara, and then adding Luis Lara again. This is not a typo. The man came back. Whatever Lara did in the interim is between him and the MayTheSchwarBeWithU front office, and we are not cleared to speculate.

**Bad MoFo's** dropped Dansby Swanson and Spencer Steer to add Brandon Marsh and Luke Raley, which is the kind of move that either signals a clear-eyed pivot toward playoff construction or a general feeling that something had to change. Also added Ranger Suárez, who is now pitching for Boston, apparently. Life comes at you fast.

**Bip Roberts** dropped Jose Altuve. No replacement was added. The move was made, the roster slot remained open, and Bip Roberts still went 8–1–1. File this under "problems the rest of the league would like to have."

**L-A-Loosh** dropped Garrett Whitlock to add a Colorado outfielder. This is the kind of transaction you make in June when you've decided the summer is already over, or when you're playing a very long game that the rest of the league hasn't figured out yet. With L-A-Loosh, both remain plausible.

**The Chicago Way**, **Grand Theft Votto**, and **Section 34** each made one quiet, sensible add. Nothing to see here, and we mean that as a compliment.
