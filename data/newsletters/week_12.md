# 40s and Blunts Weekly Rolling Coverage
### Week 12 · The one where Bell Biv Devo discovered the waiver wire and could not stop

---

**MATCHUP OF THE WEEK**

Yahoo designated **Stone Jack Ballers def. The Chicago Way, 6–4** as the matchup of the week, and honestly, fair enough. The Chicago Way entered this one sitting second in the standings and left it sitting second in the standings but slightly angrier about it. Stone Jack took the pitching side completely — ERA, WHIP, QS, SV+H — plus HR and RBI, which meant The Chicago Way's wins in R, SB, OPS, and K were decorative. Beating the second-place team on the strength of your rotation is either a statement win or a scheduling gift, depending on whom you ask. Stone Jack Ballers would like to be asked.

---

**LAST WEEK'S RESULTS**

**Chief Noc-A-Homa def. Section 34, 7–3.** Chief Noc-A-Homa took seven categories, including the customary sweep of the pitching ratios and, unusually, SB and HR on top of it. Section 34 salvaged RBI, OPS, and ERA, which is a respectable consolation prize and not much else. The commissioner's team looks like a commissioner's team. Draw your own conclusions about that.

**Bell Biv Devo def. Bad MoFo's, 6–4.** Bell Biv Devo won HR, RBI, OPS, K, QS, and SV+H. Bad MoFo's won R, SB, ERA, and WHIP — the precise four categories you'd expect a team to win when it has nothing else going right. Bad MoFo's currently sit fourth in the standings, which is quietly one of the more confusing facts about this league, and this week did nothing to explain it.

**Bip Roberts def. Old Fashioned, 8–1–1.** Bip Roberts won eight categories. Old Fashioned won one — Quality Starts, because the pitchers are apparently still clocking in even if the manager isn't. SV+H tied, which means someone on Old Fashioned's roster got a hold last week, heroically, without instruction. The 8–1–1 line is one of the more lopsided of the season, and yet it still feels like beating a parked car.

**Grand Theft Votto def. MayTheSchwarBeWithU, 5–5.** A dead heat resolved by tiebreaker into a win for Grand Theft Votto. They took SB, K, ERA, WHIP, and QS; MayTheSchwarBeWithU took R, HR, RBI, OPS, and SV+H. Two perfectly balanced halves of a fantasy baseball matchup, decided by something neither manager controlled. Nothing questionable here except the tiebreaker format itself.

**Stone Jack Ballers def. The Chicago Way, 6–4.** See above.

**Thurgood Jenkins def. L-A-Loosh, 8–2.** Thurgood Jenkins won eight categories and is now seventh in the standings, which tells you something about how unforgiving this league's schedule has been. L-A-Loosh took SB and K, which is a very Loosh way to lose — contributing real production in two categories while the rest of the roster sleeps. It is June. This is traditional. The league pretends not to notice.

**QUESTIONABLE ACTIVITY**

The story this week belongs almost entirely to **Bell Biv Devo**, who approached the waiver wire with the energy of someone reorganizing their apartment at 2 a.m. The highlights: dropped Shota Imanaga, added Jeffrey Springs, then re-added Shota Imanaga within the same week (dropping Eugenio Suárez to make room, which suggests the Springs experiment concluded quickly). Also dropped Willy Adames, then added Willy Adames back. Also dropped J.T. Realmuto in favor of Gabriel Moreno. Also dropped Willy Adames's presence was apparently reconsidered at least once during all of this. In total, Bell Biv Devo made eight transactions, several of which appear to cancel each other out. They went 6–4 on the week, so perhaps this is a method.

**MayTheSchwarBeWithU** made five moves, cycling through a small rotation of pitchers and middle infielders with the quiet urgency of someone trying to solve a puzzle they've misplaced a piece of. Luis Lara was dropped and then re-added. Noah Cameron was dropped and then re-added. The roster looks approximately the same as it did Sunday morning.

**Bad MoFo's** added Ranger Suárez, Luke Raley, and Brandon Marsh while clearing out Spencer Steer, Dansby Swanson, and Dennis Santana. Three real, purposeful upgrades in a week. The transactions make sense. The 6–4 loss happened anyway.

**The Chicago Way** added Ezequiel Duran and Colton Cowser, dropped Bryson Stott. Efficient, directional, no drama. Very on-brand.

**Bip Roberts** dropped Jose Altuve. No replacement was logged in the data available. We note this without comment.

**L-A-Loosh** dropped Garrett Whitlock for Cole Carrigg. A reliever for an outfielder, in June, while sitting eleventh. Sure.

**Old Fashioned** made no moves. As previously established, this is no longer questionable. It is simply the climate.
