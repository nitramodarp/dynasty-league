# 40s and Blunts Weekly Rolling Coverage
### Week 12 · The one where Bad MoFo's are in fourth place, apparently

---

**MATCHUP OF THE WEEK**

Yahoo designated Stone Jack Ballers' 6–4 defeat of The Chicago Way as the matchup of the week, and on inspection, the designation holds. The Chicago Way entered this matchup in second place, which is where well-run organizations go to be reminded that Stone Jack Ballers will ERA/WHIP their way into any result they want. Stone Jack swept all three pitching categories — ERA, WHIP, and QS — while also taking HR, RBI, and SV+H. The Chicago Way countered by winning R, SB, OPS, and K, which is a fine collection of categories to win when you're not trying to lose. The gap was two categories. It is the kind of loss that doesn't feel like a crisis until you check the standings and remember second place has feelings too.

---

**LAST WEEK'S RESULTS**

**Chief Noc-A-Homa def. Section 34, 7–3.** The commissioner swept the pitching column again — K, WHIP, QS, SV+H — and added three hitting categories on top of it. Section 34 salvaged RBI, OPS, and ERA, which amounts to a decent consolation prize. The ratios remain the structural foundation of this franchise. There is no sign this changes.

**Bell Biv Devo def. Bad MoFo's, 6–4.** In a week that will require some reprocessing on the part of the league's actuaries, Bell Biv Devo won. Bad MoFo's took R, SB, ERA, and WHIP — the pitching ratios, notably — while Bell Biv Devo ran the table on power and production. The ERA and WHIP split against a team sitting ninth in the standings is the kind of detail that makes you stare at a spreadsheet for longer than is professionally advisable.

**Bip Roberts def. Old Fashioned, 8–1–1.** Bip took eight categories, tied one, and left one on the table. The one Old Fashioned won was QS. The QS category does not appear to have been contested so much as ceded and then quietly accepted by both parties. Bip Roberts is fifth in the standings and seemingly unbothered by this.

**Grand Theft Votto def. MayTheSchwarBeWithU, 5–5.** A tie result rendered as a win by tiebreaker, which seems like exactly how this franchise would prefer to acquire victories. Grand Theft Votto took the pitching side — SB, K, ERA, WHIP, QS — and MayTheSchwarBeWithU won the hitting side plus saves. A perfectly even house, divided.

**Stone Jack Ballers def. The Chicago Way, 6–4.** See above.

**Thurgood Jenkins def. L-A-Loosh, 8–2.** Thurgood swept eight categories and held L-A-Loosh to SB and K. This is the sort of performance that, in any other month, would feel like a statement. In June, when L-A-Loosh is involved, it reads more like a footnote. L-A-Loosh is eleven games under .500 in the standings and has nine championship banners. These facts coexist without apparent tension.

**PLAYOFF PICTURE**

Through twelve weeks, the top six are occupied and all of them have meaningful separation from the cut line. Chief Noc-A-Homa sits seventeen categories clear of the bubble, The Chicago Way fourteen, and Stone Jack Ballers ten and a half. Even Bad MoFo's — fourth place, a sentence that still requires a moment — holds a six-game cushion. Bip Roberts and Section 34 round out the field at five and four clear, respectively. Ten weeks remain, which is roughly one hundred category decisions, so none of this is settled. But the six teams inside the line are not in immediate distress.

Below the cut, Thurgood Jenkins is four back of Section 34's sixth seed following an 8–2 week that should have felt like progress and mostly did. Grand Theft Votto is six back. Bell Biv Devo, MayTheSchwarBeWithU, and L-A-Loosh are each eight and a half back, which at this stage of the season is a lot of ground to make up while also finishing at the same time. Old Fashioned is fifteen back. No team has been formally eliminated. Some situations are more theoretical than others.

**QUESTIONABLE ACTIVITY**

Bell Biv Devo opened the week at 4:16 AM on Tuesday by adding Gabriel Moreno and dropping Clayton Beeter. Later that same morning, J.T. Realmuto was dropped. The catcher position was apparently restructured before most of the league had made coffee. Then, on Thursday morning, Willy Adames was added — and by Sunday he was gone, replaced by Taylor Ward. In between, Bell Biv Devo added Shota Imanaga, dropped Shota Imanaga one minute later, and then added Jeffrey Springs. The Imanaga tenure lasted sixty seconds. There may be an explanation. It has not been provided.

MayTheSchwarBeWithU operated with similar velocity. Noah Cameron was added Friday morning and dropped Sunday morning in favor of Michael McGreevy. Luis Lara arrived Friday and was gone by Sunday in favor of Blaze Alexander. Kaelen Culpepper was added; Dylan Crews was dropped. This constitutes four transactions in roughly forty-eight hours, none of which appear to be connected to each other by any visible strategic thread.

Bad MoFo's made three moves Friday afternoon — Ranger Suarez, Brandon Marsh, and Luke Raley in; Dennis Santana, Dansby Swanson, and Spencer Steer out. This is either a deliberate roster reset or what a roster reset looks like when you're in fourth place and briefly confused about it.

The Chicago Way added Colton Cowser and Ezequiel Duran in back-to-back moves Thursday morning, dropping Bryson Stott in the process. Focused, sequential, purposeful. Almost jarring in context.

L-A-Loosh dropped Garrett Whitlock to add Cole Carrigg. This is the kind of move that is very difficult to evaluate in June and very easy to evaluate in October.
