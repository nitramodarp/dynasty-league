# 40s and Blunts Weekly Rolling Coverage
### Week 12 · The one where three teams clinched and one team was already gone

---

**MATCHUP OF THE WEEK**

**Stone Jack Ballers def. The Chicago Way, 6–4.** Yahoo called this the Matchup of the Week, and for once the algorithm earns its keep. The Chicago Way — quiet, efficient, currently holding the second seed — ran into a Stone Jack team that took the pitching categories wholesale: ERA, WHIP, QS, and SV+H. Chicago won the speed and strikeout side and still lost. The lesson, as always with Stone Jack, is that if you let him own your ratios, you've already lost the argument.

---

**LAST WEEK'S RESULTS**

**Chief Noc-A-Homa def. Section 34, 7–3.** The commissioner swept seven categories, ceded RBI, OPS, and ERA, and walked away with another comfortable win. He took SB, which is notable primarily because he doesn't always do that, and the league should probably not let him get comfortable doing it. Section 34 won ERA and still lost by four categories. That's a rough way to find out your pitching week went to waste.

**Bell Biv Devo def. Bad MoFo's, 6–4.** Bell Biv Devo took HR, RBI, OPS, K, QS, and SV+H. Bad MoFo's won the ratios and the counting-stat-on-the-basepaths column — R and SB — which is an unusual profile for a team that finished four categories short. The effort was organized. The result was not.

**Bip Roberts def. Old Fashioned, 8–1–1.** Bip took eight categories. Old Fashioned won Quality Starts. They tied on saves and holds, which, all things considered, is probably the highlight of Old Fashioned's week. The roster's continued existence remains a matter of faith rather than evidence.

**Grand Theft Votto def. MayTheSchwarBeWithU, 5–5.** Technically a win on tiebreaker. Grand Theft Votto took the pitching side plus SB and K; MayTheSchwarBeWithU countered with R, HR, RBI, OPS, and SV+H. This is the fantasy equivalent of a split decision that leaves both fighters feeling like they probably should have won.

**Stone Jack Ballers def. The Chicago Way, 6–4.** See above. Already covered. Moving on.

**Thurgood Jenkins def. L-A-Loosh, 8–2.** Thurgood walked off with R, HR, RBI, OPS, ERA, WHIP, QS, and SV+H. L-A-Loosh claimed SB and K and not much else. Eight categories is a dominant week by anyone's measure, and it's worth noting L-A-Loosh has now lost ground at precisely the wrong time of year — though if history is any guide, the league will spend the next three months pretending that's irrelevant.

**POWER RANKINGS**

1. **Chief Noc-A-Homa** — 72–43–5, clinched, and apparently not interested in letting Section 34 have nice things.
2. **The Chicago Way** — Also clinched, which is cold comfort after losing the Matchup of the Week to the commissioner's brother.
3. **Stone Jack Ballers** — Clinched as well. The family now owns three of the top three seeds, which is a sentence the rest of the league should find mildly alarming.
4. **Bad MoFo's** — Six games clear of the cut line, in position, and still finding new ways to lose to Bell Biv Devo.
5. **Bip Roberts** — Five clear, dropped José Altuve, won eight categories. An efficient week in every direction.
6. **Section 34** — Four clear of the bubble, which is comfortable until it isn't. Currently holding the last guaranteed playoff spot.
7. **Thurgood Jenkins** — Four back of sixth, which stings more given that 8–2 week. A win like that deserves better than "still outside the line."
8. **Grand Theft Votto** — Six back. Won a 5–5 on tiebreaker, which is the standings equivalent of finding a dollar in an old coat.
9. **Bell Biv Devo** — Beat Bad MoFo's and still sit 8.5 out. The math is unkind.
10. **MayTheSchwarBeWithU** — Also 8.5 back, also 50–64. This week's transactions suggest a team that has noticed the situation.
11. **L-A-Loosh** — Nine titles. Currently 8.5 out of a playoff spot. The bear is not sleeping — the bear just lost to Thurgood Jenkins by six categories.
12. **Old Fashioned** — Eliminated. The standings finally said what the rest of us have been thinking since April.

**PLAYOFF PICTURE**

Through twelve weeks, the top three are clinched — Chief Noc-A-Homa, The Chicago Way, and Stone Jack Ballers have their spots locked. Bad MoFo's, Bip Roberts, and Section 34 are in but not clinched, sitting four to six games clear of the cut line with ten weeks remaining. Below the line, Thurgood Jenkins is the closest at four back and, after an 8–2 week, is the most credible threat to push someone out. Grand Theft Votto is six back and in the conversation. Bell Biv Devo, MayTheSchwarBeWithU, and L-A-Loosh are each 8.5 out and running low on runway. Old Fashioned has been formally eliminated, which changes very little in practical terms.

**QUESTIONABLE ACTIVITY**

The week began, appropriately, at 4 AM Tuesday, when **Bell Biv Devo** added Gabriel Moreno and dropped Clayton Beeter. Fine. Normal. Then, at 7:58 AM — less than four hours later — Bell Biv Devo dropped J.T. Realmuto. That's two catchers out the door before the morning commute was over. By Friday, Bell Biv Devo had added Willy Adames and then, by Sunday, dropped him — a 72-hour relationship with a shortstop that presumably ended amicably. The week concluded with Bell Biv Devo adding Shota Imanaga at 11:42 AM Sunday and dropping him at 11:43 AM Sunday. One minute. Imanaga was on this roster for sixty seconds. Whatever he did to earn that drop, it was fast.

**MayTheSchwarBeWithU** made five moves on Friday and Sunday, including adding Noah Cameron on Saturday morning and replacing him with Michael McGreevy by Sunday — a starting pitcher carousel that lasted approximately 44 hours. Luis Lara arrived Saturday and was gone by Sunday as well. The activity is noted; the results from this week's matchup, a 5–5 tiebreaker loss, suggest the urgency was not misplaced, just not quite sufficient.

**Bad MoFo's** made three moves in a two-minute window Friday afternoon — Ranger Suárez, Brandon Marsh, and Luke Raley all arrived while Santana, Swanson, and Steer headed out. Three simultaneous additions before 3 PM on a Friday has a specific energy to it.

**Bip Roberts** quietly dropped José Altuve on Wednesday morning and said nothing further about it. Which is somehow more unsettling than any of the above.
